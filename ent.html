Art-preneurship involves initiating and managing a supply chain which involves production and marketing of art crafts made by rural women, teaching unemployed rural women how to make simple art products which are in demand in the urban market like cards, envelopes, terrariums etc. and finally to facilitate the marketing of these products.
The purpose of such an activity is to initiate and manage a chain supply which involves production of art products such as cards, envelopes, terrariums by rural women and connect them to a market so that they benefit. The idea is to teach these rural, unemployed women to make simple art products which are in demand in the urban market. The key aspect addressed will be to teach women on how to make such products in the most economical way possible using rudimentary materials.
This also involves identifying potential markets for each product and linking them with these groups of women.





Prototype Challenge


Prototype Challenge is an event that provides a platform to showcase ideas for products and services which have potential to start-up. Anyone and everyone with an idea properly executed and who wish to launch their product in the market can participate in this and present their ideas to a panel and the public.
Providing quality mentorship for the participants and sessions on the intricacies of starting up. It creates opportunities and encourages young talent to convert their prototype model into products. Students with properly executed ideas, who wish to launch their product in the market, get a chance to present their ideas to panel and public.
